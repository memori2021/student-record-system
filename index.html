OF BARANGAY SAN ROQUE CASTILLA SORSOGON

HISTORY

Historically, somewhere 1924 the residents of Cumadcad and Salvacion where their houses are near the boundary of theses two joining barangay, they agree to form another barangay. It was headed by Lorenzo Llaneta, Igmedio Millena, and Apolonio Lleva. Apolonio Lleva donated a part of his land then Lorenzo Llaneta donated the image of “St. Roque.” With the efforts of this three and the cooperation of other residents they were able to built a chapel that every people from nearly places were also attended all the activities conducted in this place until they used to call it “SAN ROQUE.” Barangay San Roque is more or less 388 hectares which bounded from North of Barangay Salvacion, South is Barangay Cumadcad, East is Barangay San Vicente, and West is the Barangay Cagbacong, Legazpi City. San Roque is a barangay in the municipality of Castilla located at province of Sorsogon on its first district. Its population as determined by the 2020 Census was 703. This represented 1.16% of the total population of Castilla.
